T1 mapping¶

The MRI signal in a spoiled gradient recalled echo is described by:

\[ S = M_0 \sin(\alpha) \frac{1 - \exp(-TR/T_1)}{1 - \cos(\alpha) \exp(-TR/T_1)} \exp(-TE/T_2) \]

The acquisition parameters are the flip angle \(\alpha\), repetition time \(TR\) and echo time \(TE\)—these are controlled by the scanner and are known1. The tissue-specific parameters are the magnetization \(M_0\) and spin-lattice relaxation time \(T_1\).

1

Let’s ignore B1 effects for now.
